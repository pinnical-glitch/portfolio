005 — JOURNEY

I took things apart until only one habit was left.

Five years. Dozens of builds thrown back. One instinct wouldn't die: crack every surface open until the gears underneath show themselves.

01

Somebody Else's Problems

A booking system for a vet clinic. A storefront for a clothing brand. Appointment slots, patient records, payment rails — none of it glamorous, all of it unforgiving. No tutorial ever taught what a 2am client email teaches in one sentence. This is where the training wheels came off.

02

The Architecture Click

Something snapped into focus somewhere around line 3,000. One model, one source of truth, every chart and forecast fed from the same beating core — no duplicated state, no quiet lies between components. A discipline learned on spreadsheets that would, without asking permission, rebuild itself inside a physics engine.

03

The Rejection

Every CS portfolio online started looking like the same portfolio. Same to-do list. Same weather widget. Same dashboard, wearing a different font. Deleted the draft. Went looking for a problem ugly enough, old enough, and stubborn enough to actually fight back.

04

The Kelvin Build

Eight months arguing with 19th-century physics until it agreed to run in a browser tab. First-principles electrostatics, a charge-transfer model built from nothing, particles rendered frame by frame until the spark-gap broke exactly where it should. Nobody else has shipped this. Not once, not anywhere.

05

What's Next

Electrical engineering. Code was the rehearsal — copper, silicon, and current are the real stage. Everything here was practice for a room with a soldering iron in it. This portfolio is the door. The lab is what's on the other side.

MECHANISM

Why it shouldn't
work as well as it does.

Lord Kelvin's dropper is a beautiful cheat. Two streams of water fall through grounded induction rings. Statistical noise gives one side a fraction more charge; that charge induces the opposite sign onto the drops falling through that side; those drops land in a can wired to the other side's ring — strengthening the very imbalance that created them.

The loop is regenerative. Charge begets charge. Within seconds the potential between the collector spheres reaches thousands of volts and the air itself gives way in a spark, resetting the system to begin again. The simulation models that transfer honestly — no faked numbers, just the feedback math running live.

Home Energy Resilience Planner

Open the live build ↗

THE MODEL

Resilience you can
see before it fails.

Most homeowners discover their energy resilience only when the grid goes dark. This planner inverts that moment: you declare your solar array, your battery bank, and your typical draw, and it forecasts — to the minute — how long you remain powered, and precisely where the shortfalls land.

The dashboard is pure inputs-to-outcomes. Every slider re-runs the same deterministic simulation the visualization reads from, so chart and figure can never disagree. Same instinct as the Kelvin dropper — render the invisible system legible before it bites.

Budget Flow started as a single question — where does the money actually go? It grew into a 4,000-line instrument: transaction import, categorization, and forecasting, all driven by one state model every view reads from.

That inputs-to-dashboard pattern became the default. When the Home Energy Resilience Planner came later, it reused the same shape — one source of truth, many views, nothing duplicated. This project isn't here for its charts; it's the skeleton every later build stands on.
